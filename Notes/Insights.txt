Revenue is highly concentrated in a few key regions, with Riyadh and Jeddah leading by a significant margin.

Revenue is not only driven by the number of transactions, but also by the type of transaction and fixed income sources (Revenue doesn’t always increase with more transactions)

Operational costs remain relatively consistent across regions, regardless of revenue differences.

ATM maintenance cost is the highest among all AMC components, followed by VSAT, while UPS has the lowest contribution.

While ATM AMC is the highest maintenance cost, it remains lower than cash replenishment (CRA), indicating that cash handling is the primary cost driver.

Most operational costs are driven by site maintenance rather than equipment repairs.

Some regions show a higher number of underperforming ATMs based on uptime.

Riyadh and Jeddah generate the highest profits despite their high operational costs, with profit margins reaching up to 100%.

Despite not being a top-performing region overall, Tabuk dominates individual ATM performance, with 6 out of the top 10 ATMs, including the top 3.

Jeddah and Makkah record the highest number of ATM downtime occurrences, with repeated cases across multiple months, indicating persistent operational issues.
